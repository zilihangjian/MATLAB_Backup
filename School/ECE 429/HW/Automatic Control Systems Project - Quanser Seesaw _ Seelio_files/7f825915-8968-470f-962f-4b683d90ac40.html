1. Executive summary In this project, we has developed a controller that can effectively balance the Quanser Seesaw system. The Quanser Seesaw system is comprised of a cart driven by DC motor and a seesaw where the cart could move along. The voltage input to the DC motor is controlled by the controller, and the cart position and seesaw’s angular position is measured as feedback information. We implement a lead controller for the cart control and a lead-integration controller for the overall system balance.
The design procedure could be broken down to several steps. First, the dynamics of the seesaw system is modeled and simplified to a linear system. Based on the results, a Simulink model is developed to simulate the response of the seesaw system. The second step is to design a cart controller using root locus technique. The cart controller is tested and tuned on the actual seesaw-cart system. Next we designed two lead controllers in series plus an integral controller in parallel to balance the whole system. After tuning the parameters in simulation as well as in the actual system, our controller can balance the seesaw with more than 20 washers added on one side. The details of the design process and results are presented in the following sections of this report.
2. System Modeling
2.1. Introduction The seesaw scale system is composed of a seesaw and a cart that is powered by a DC motor and moves along the track on the seesaw. The system is shown in Figure 1.
Figure 1 Seesaw System
There are two encoders in the system. One encoder is mounted on the wheel of cart to measure the rotation of the wheel and thus would be able to give us the displacement of the cart on the track. The other encoder is located on the seesaw joint which could measure the angular displacement of the seesaw system. To model the complete dynamics of the system, it is divided into three parts: motor dynamics; cart dynamics and seesaw dynamic.
